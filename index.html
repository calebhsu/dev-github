is to actually meet you at GitHub and
pick a name (a good one, because this could end up on a resume). And that's all you need (aside from the confirmation email, but you already know that).
Click around, get familiar with your empty profile (it won't be like that for long!) and keep reading...

Your first repository

can seem daunting but let's start at the basics. Click the + icon in the upper right and
enter a reasonable repo name (short, so you it's easier for you to get to know Git ater on). Let's
initialize with a README, which can be anything from blurb stub to almost-wiki. By the way, if you ever wanted to meet Markdown, now is the time.) And create repository!
